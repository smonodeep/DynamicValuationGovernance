## Abstract
Automated valuation models (AVMs) are increasingly used in property-backed lending to support collateral valuation and credit risk assessment.
While prior research has examined AVM accuracy, predictive performance, and model bias, less attention has been paid to
how valuation uncertainty generated by such models is incorporated into institutional credit risk decision-making, 
particularly in heterogeneous and data-constrained property markets.

This research proposes a governance-oriented framework positioning valuation governance as the institutional transmission mechanism linking valuation outputs to downstream credit risk analytics,
including loan-to-value (LTV) calibration, probability of default (PD), loss given default (LGD), and expected credit loss (ECL) estimation.
The study combines conceptual framework development with a multi-stage empirical investigation examining price formation,
valuation mispricing, adjustment dynamics, and distributional uncertainty across selected Indian urban property markets.

The empirical component will utilize quarterly datasets constructed from Reserve Bank of India (RBI) House Price Index (HPI) series,
rental market indicators, and monetary policy variables. 
Quantitative methods including regression analysis, quantile regression, and 
volatility modeling will be applied to examine how valuation uncertainty behaves across different market conditions and regimes.
The research is explicitly situated within the domain of governance in collateral-based lending rather than 
enterprise-level capital management or macroeconomic forecasting.
The expected contribution is the development of an uncertainty-aware valuation governance framework capable of improving
institutional interpretation, escalation mechanisms, 
and credit risk decision-making in illiquid and structurally heterogeneous property markets.
